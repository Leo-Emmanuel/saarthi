# Math Dictation Pronunciation Guide

The system uses natural language parsing to convert your spoken math into beautifully formatted LaTeX expressions. While it is quite flexible, using these specific phrases will guarantee the best results. 

## 1. Basic Arithmetic & Algebra
* **Addition:** Say `"x plus y"` → `x + y`
* **Subtraction:** Say `"x minus y"` → `x - y`
* **Multiplication:** Say `"x times y"` or `"x multiplied by y"` → `x \times y` or `x \cdot y`
* **Division/Fraction:** Say `"a over b"` or `"a divided by b"` → `\frac{a}{b}`
* **Equations:** Say `"x equals y"` → `x = y`

## 2. Exponents and Powers
* **Squared / Power of 2:** Say `"x squared"` or `"x square"` → `x²`
* **Cubed / Power of 3:** Say `"y cubed"` or `"y cube"` → `y³`
* **Arbitrary Powers:** Say `"z to the power of 4"` or `"two to the 5"` → `z^{4}`, `2^{5}`
* **Exponential:** Say `"e to the x"` → `e^{x}`

## 3. Roots
* **Square Root:** Say `"square root of x"` or `"root x"` → `\sqrt{x}`
* **Cube Root:** Say `"cube root of y"` → `\sqrt[3]{y}`
* **Nth Root:** Say `"nth root of z"` → `\sqrt[n]{z}`

## 4. Grouping & Brackets
When terms are complicated, tell the system to group them:
* **Quantities:** Say `"quantity a plus b end quantity"` → `(a + b)`
* **Parentheses:** Say `"open bracket x plus y close bracket"` → `(x + y)`
* **Whole Squared:** Say `"x plus y whole squared"` → `(x + y)²`

## 5. Functions & Trigonometry
* **Trig Functions:** Say `"sine of x"`, `"cosine of theta"`, `"tangent squared x"`
* **Inverse Trig:** Say `"inverse sine of y"`, `"arc tangent of z"`, `"sine inverse of x"`
* **Logarithms:** 
  * Say `"log of x"` → `\log x`
  * Say `"natural log of y"` or `"ln of y"` → `\ln y`
  * Say `"log base 2 of 8"` → `\log_{2} 8`

## 6. Calculus
* **Derivatives:** 
  * Say `"derivative of x squared with respect to x"` or `"d by d x of x squared"`
  * Say `"second derivative of y"`
  * Say `"partial derivative of f with respect to x"`
* **Integrals:** 
  * **Indefinite:** Say `"integral of x"`
  * **Definite:** Say `"integral from 0 to 1 of x squared"`
  * **Double/Triple:** Say `"double integral from a to b of f"`
  
## 7. Summations and Products
* **Summation:** Say `"sum from n equals 1 to 10 of n squared"`
* **Product:** Say `"product from i equals 1 to n of x"`

## 8. Statistics & Combinatorics
* **Combinations/Permutations:** Say `"n choose k"` or `"combination n k"`, `"n p r"`
* **Variables:** Say `"x bar"`, `"y tilde"`, `"magnitude of v"`
* **Expected Value / Variance:** Say `"expected value of x"`, `"variance of y"`

## 9. Greek Letters
Pronounce them normally: `"alpha"`, `"beta"`, `"gamma"`, `"pi"`, `"theta"`. 
* For capital letters, just add "capital": `"capital delta"`, `"capital sigma"`.

## 10. Sets & Logic
* **Relations:** Say `"greater than or equal to"`, `"not equal to"`, `"proportional to"`
* **Set Theory:** Say `"belongs to"`, `"union"`, `"intersection"`, `"empty set"`
* **Logic:** Say `"for all"`, `"there exists"`, `"implies"`, `"if and only if"`

---
**Tip:** If the system misunderstands your dictation, try using the **"Undo"** / **"Clear Step"** commands and speaking the expression slightly slower with emphasis on keywords like "over", "squared", or "quantity".
